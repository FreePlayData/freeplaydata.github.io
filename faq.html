Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

What is Freeplay?

Freeplay Data is a consulting service available on a contract basis to provide artificial intelligence solutions in the general areas of machine learning, data science, vision, natural language processing, planning and scheduling, and predictive analytics.

Freeplay consists of one principal employee, Eric Hsu, and maintains the capability of staffing additional experts according to project requirements.

What kinds of problems does Freeplay solve?

In the abstract, Freeplay is well-suited for any problem where a business (or consumer, or client, or government institution) faces a regular series of decisions among multiple courses of action, perhaps with incomplete information, and it is valuable to optimize the overall outcome over the long run, even as the optimal choices are constantly changing with the environment.  Concrete examples might include: forecasting sales and managing inventory based on historical data and real-time indicators; recommending products or services that are most likely to interest an individual consumer based on online data; finding workflow or supply chain inefficiencies using sensors and transactions data; or optimizing the flow of traffic through a city.

Additionally, an artificial intelligence company like Freeplay can enable new products and capabilities based on advancing modalities like machine vision and natural language interaction.  For instance, such techniques can be used to predict what types of products someone prefers by studying their photos, or to detect fraud or plagiarism by analyzing patterns of speech or writing.

How can Freeplay solve my problem?

Freeplay can provide a wide range of services, ranging over: advising a new organization on technical architecture and the search for talent; learning an enterprise line of business and conducting a study or analysis in support of current operations or technology roadmap; evaluating off-the-shelf methods to develop proof-of-concept implementations for new features and products; and, developing custom algorithms and systems to solve problems and take opportunities that have not been attempted before.

Because our memership is not only academically trained but has some industrial experience working on legitimate large-scale, highly-available, distributed production systems , Freeplay has the ability to remaining cognizant of real-world test and deployment considerations while conducting research and development, and can also work together with clients on the implementation part of the integration process.

In general, no form of collaboration is unworthy of consideration; for most cases, though, the simplest initial partnership is most likely a study or a prototype and demo.

Technical Questions

What are Freeplay Data's specific areas of expertise?

With sufficient time, Freeplay is capable of evaluating, improving, and delivering essentially any published Artificial Intelligence technology in solving a client's concrete problem.    This competence is based on the principal's long and varied experience across multiple areas of AI, and the ability to solicit guidance from many of the most influential AI practitioners in academia and industry.

In a more expedient scenario, Freeplay can rely on existing experience to pragmatically deliver solutions using statistical modelling and prediction, supervised/semi-supervised/unsupervised machine learning (to include up-to-date familiarity with neural network techniques and packages), reinforcement learning and control, automated planning and scheduling (to include combinatorial optimization), and decision theory.  To a lesser extent, Freeplay is also able to rely on publically available packages and data to perform natural language processing or to design sensor networks.  Generally, though, software systems are a stronger internal capability than hardware systems, and thus areas like robotics and internet-of-things would require learning on the job and/or additional staffingere's no job that's we won't consider!

Most generally, though, the opportunity to work as partners in developing a deep understanding of a specific business area, then defining an entirely new capability in this area, and ultimately creating a novel solution drawn from a deep understanding of existing techinques, is an understandably under-appreciated form of collaboration.  Freeplay appreciates the unwieldly nature of defining and requesting features that are not known to exist, and that for even the most extravagantly technology-driven organizations the bottom line calls for transaction even as investors, the public, and destiny clamor for transformation.  So, we are willing to work on anything that provides value, but it is the chance to partner in understaning a business and identifying opportunities for innovation that take best advantage of Freeplay's most distinguishing competencies.

What is Freeplay's key distinguishing form of expertise?

By the self-assessment of its principal, Eric Hsu, Freeplay's most distinguishing competence lies in combining a deep and independent understanding of traditionally academic artificial intelligence techniques with a similarly deep understanding of its increasingly industrial application areas, to work with partners in inventing new products and capabilities.

Such depth of experience produces strong informed opinions about AI (for instance, that "machine learning is really about modelling and optimization, and the vast majority of publications, even those that present modelling as a key contribution, are really about incremental improvements in optimization, while few are able contribute a signficant innovation in modelling.") and business (like, that "the true focus of automation in industry has not been on productivity or scale or human betterment but the market incentive to commodify labor-based processes into technology-based assets for investment; you can make some money by working or employing people but you can only earn arbitrarily large multiples from a sector by owning something.")  Such ideological insights, in addition to appearing unduly presumptuous when stated thusly, are neither entirely original and unique, within their subject areas, nor widely accepted or even tolerated.  However, in subjects of intense recent or longstanding interest such as high technology, artificial intelligence, and making money, such a historical perspective—however subjective—can be critical in overcoming hype and superficial understanding to identify true opportunities.

Less abstractly, one more founder's opinion is that he is "absolutely positive" that he is the world's #1 expert on a particular way of Integrating Probabilistic Reasoning with Constraint Satisfaction .

Is Freeplay familiar with Technology X or Programming Language Y?

Freeplay is confident in its ability to use the best tool for any job, to include expeditiously evaluating new frameworks, or learning new languages as needed.  That said, clients may have legacy requirements that affect the consideration of what is the best tool for the job.  With that in mind it may be worth noting that the main areas of past experience center on Scala, Java, Python, C/C++, Lisp, Ruby, and Matlab implementations running on Unix-type operating systems, to include various software frameworks like Spark, Pig, Hadoop, Kafka, Oozie, various Message-Queing sytems like ZeroMQ, NoSQL implementations like MongoDB, etc.  Areas of lesser experience would include development for Windows and Android or for proprietary platforms like Salesforce or Oracle.

Company Information

What is the origin of the company?

As of December 2017, Freeplay Data is a brand new business!   The company has only one member, Eric Hsu—he has twenty-plus years of experience applying Artificial Intelligence techniques to specific problems in the start-up, enterprise, and government research sectors.   (A history of previous positions is listed on LinkedIn.)  

The primary assumption in founding this company is that external clients will present unique, challenging, and well-defined problems, but will not always have the appropriate requirements for internal staffing.   For Freeplay, this is also a way to ensure access to projects with high-impact outcomes, strong commitment from the business, and well-defined timelines, in comparison to working as an internal employee for a single firm.  For more technology-driven organizations, the ideal relationship would include even the opportunity to collaborate on defining new capabilities and inventing new solutions that do not yet exist anywhere in a given industry.

What are your rates?

Freeplay will work on a contract basis, with rates dependent on the nature of the project; please get in touch to work out a project-specific estimate!   One rule of thumb is that a new feature or study should cost about as much as a car—a used or low-performance stock car may have very little cost, and is sometimes enough to get the job done; custom or exotic cars designed to high performance standards may be many times more valuable and costly.  In most cases, advice is free!

Where is the company located?

Freeplay Data is based in beautiful Vancouver, Canada.
