Setting Clients developing GPU-enhanced video camera need an onboard facial recognition platform to realize multiple applications.

Requirements Processing and memory are constrained by camera hardware; software can be trained offline but must provide application services in real time:

  • In access control setting, camera compares live video of a person seeking entry with stored ID photo.
  • In detection setting, platform uses person of interest's photo to search streaming video for matches.
  • Additionally, client requires original research into novel capabilities that will differentiate product from existing state of the art.


Work Performed Evaluated publically-available convolutional neural networks for facial recognition, then trained custom networks to resolve shortcomings on client data; designed additional features in response to unanticipated problems and opportunities:

  • Secondary classification feature allows platform to learn from mistakes and prevent repeated misclassification due to difficult images, classes of images, and environmental conditions.
  • Object tracking feature extends single-image processing to follow the trajectory of an object across multiple frames of video.
  • Attribute prediction system, given single photo of a person, can predict consumer preferences, ad conversion rate, and demographic orientation.   Depicted above: demonstration for collaborators at University of British Columbia, differentiating representations of Ksitigarbha in religious art, either as Dizang in China, or Jizo in Japan; two large composite faces uncover underlying structure of each embodiment.

Outcome In limited deployment, secondary classifier reduces repeat mislabeling to 0.001%; object tracker matches state of the art assessed by public competition; attribute predictor more than quadruples conversion rate in test setting, and achieves 81% accuracy in predicting whether a Twitter user is a follower of Donald Trump or Hillary Clinton based solely on their user photo.
